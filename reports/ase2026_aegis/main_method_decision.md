# Main Method Decision

## Evidence review
- **AEGIS hierarchy** – `reports/ase2026_aegis/table_main.csv` shows the full manager variant topping out at 0.50 success with negative reward (‑5.66) and 14.3 macro steps. Flattened/reduced controllers collapse to 0.0 success, and only the “no constraints” ablation reaches 1.0 because it disables the very budgets the paper studies. Stage‑B pretraining traces (`results/aegis_rl/aegis_reduced_run/reports/stage_b_summary.json`) remain low-signal, so AEGIS cannot beat even the heuristic baseline.
- **TARL (teacher-guided residual learning)** – `reports/ase2026_aegis/tarl_table_main.csv` records 1.0 success with zero overrides, i.e., the policy copies the teacher exactly and never exercises its residual action space.
- **Legacy STRIDE (teacher imitation)** – Re-running the largest STRIDE suite (`scripts/run_stride_suite.py --variants stride_without_uncertainty_features stride_gate_plus_residual --episodes 32 --dataset-episodes 256 --seeds 0 1 2 3 4 --override-threshold 0.6 --residual-thresholds 0.6 0.75 0.8 0.9`) refreshed all metrics under `results/aegis_rl/` and rebuilt the tables. The teacher variant (`stride_without_uncertainty_features`) now reports 0.981 success, +61.2 reward, and zero overrides (`reports/ase2026_aegis/stride_table_main.csv`). Residual add-ons still regress: the latest gate+residual sweep averages 0.761 success with override regret 0.246 (`reports/ase2026_aegis/stride_table_ablation.csv`), meaning the extra policy fires overrides that hurt as often as they help.
- **Counterfactual STRIDE** – The branch-rollout dataset remains the only place overrides see synthetic futures. `reports/ase2026_aegis/cstride_table_main.csv` confirms that the counterfactual gate-only hybrid keeps 1.0 success with a modest 8.9 % override rate and 0.819 override win rate, offering calibrated diagnostics but no success lift. Every value/residual head again collapses to 0.60–0.92 success with override regret ≥0.74 (`reports/ase2026_aegis/cstride_table_ablation.csv`), so richer heads still overfire.
- **Funnel + dataset readiness** – The reproducible ETL audit (`data/topcoder/corpus_summary.json`, `data/topcoder/executable_subset_summary.json`, `data/topcoder/funnel_report.json`) shows we control the full 22,023-challenge corpus, yet only 3,966 challenges satisfy the runnable heuristics and none of the CGCS rows are solvable (60 total, all rejected). This confirms we cannot “cherry pick” easy wins to inflate results.
- **Real Topcoder SRM runs** – `reports/decomposition/real_world/real_repo/summary.md` (OpenAI gpt‑4.1‑mini provider) demonstrates that contract-aware strategies still fail all four SRM backlog tasks despite perfect localization precision/recall and oracle teacher pass_rate = 1.0. Clause coverage stalls near 0.5 and the logged semantic failures are aggregation/filtering regressions, reinforcing that the teacher remains the safest control policy.

## Decision
The **teacher imitation baseline remains the flagship method**. It consistently delivers ≥98 % success without overrides, TARL collapses to pure imitation, AEGIS never clears 50 % success, and every residual/value/counterfactual head that tries to exceed the teacher either regresses or overfires. Counterfactual gating is valuable instrumentation—it proves we can measure override wins (8.9 % triggers, 0.819 win rate) without hurting success—but it still lands at parity with the teacher rather than surpassing it. We will therefore headline the teacher policy, present counterfactual gating as diagnostic evidence, and frame the rest of C-STRIDE/AEGIS as negative results.

CGCS and the real-repo harness supply the semantic diagnosis: clause graphs, witnesses, and repo traces show why overrides still lose (aggregation/filtering clauses stay unsatisfied). Until counterfactual supervision spans entire repair trajectories and multi-file edits succeed on SRM repos, the honest conclusion is that calibrated teacher imitation is the safest deployable control method.
