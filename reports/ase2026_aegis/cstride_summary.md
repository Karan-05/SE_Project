# C-STRIDE Summary

- **Counterfactual supervision** – `scripts/build_counterfactual_dataset.py --episodes 256 --max-alternatives 3` generates `results/aegis_rl/counterfactual/branch_rollouts.jsonl` (4,925 states / 10,144 candidate rollouts), letting us train gates/value models on branch deltas rather than proxy labels (`summary.json`).
- **Teacher baseline** – `results/aegis_rl/cstride_cstride_imitation_only/cstride_imitation_only_summary.json` reports 1.0 success, 0% overrides, and 0.261 avg cost (`cstride_table_main.csv` row `cstride_imitation_only`), establishing the strongest imitation reference.
- **Best counterfactual variant** – `results/aegis_rl/cstride_cstride_gate_only/cstride_gate_only_summary.json` shows the counterfactual gate-only policy keeps 1.0 success with slightly higher reward (78.7 vs 72.5) and a modest 8.9% override rate whose win rate is 0.82 while regret stays 0.0. Overrides are rare but meaningful: per `stride_seed_summary.csv`, seeds 1–4 override 10–12% of manager steps with perfect win rate.
- **Override limitations** – Value/residual flavours collapse: `cstride_gate_plus_value`, `cstride_cost_aware`, and `cstride_without_uncertainty` all drop to ~0.60 success with 32% overrides and harmful override fractions ≈0.78, while `cstride_without_teacher_confidence` only reaches 0.92 success (`reports/ase2026_aegis/cstride_table_ablation.csv`).
- **Take-away** – Counterfactual gating delivers calibrated overrides without hurting the teacher, but every stacked value/residual head remains unreliable; the “best” learned option is still the gate-only imitation hybrid, not a superior controller.
